Discography

Dark Country Boy — Songs

All ten tracks from the debut album Fire in the Blood (Dark Blues & Dark Country). Raw American roots music with no studio polish and no compromises. Stream on Spotify and all major platforms.

01

Fire in the Blood

The title track and opening statement. "Dark Country Boy — Fire in the Blood" sets the album's tone from the first bar: raw guitar, gritty vocals, and a lyrical obsession with what burns inside a man and refuses to go out. Part delta blues, part outlaw declaration, this is the song that announces what Dark Country Boy is and what it isn't. No stadium echoes. No radio compromise. Just the fire and the blood that carries it.

02

Ain't Dead Yet

Dark country at its most defiant. "Dark Country Boy — Ain't Dead Yet" is a survival anthem for people who don't want survival anthems — rough-edged, unapologetic, built on the simple and profound fact that being still standing is its own kind of victory. Blues guitar that bites. Vocals that have clearly been around the block. The kind of song that hits different when you've been through something.

Outlaw Country Survival Dark Blues
▶ Stream
03

Ashes Ain't the End

What rises from destruction. "Ashes Ain't the End" takes the blues tradition of finding light in darkness and plants it squarely in dark country soil. There's grief in this track — real grief, not movie-grief — but there's also the stubborn insistence that grief isn't the last word. One of the album's most emotionally direct moments and one of the best Dark Country Boy songs for listeners who've lost something significant.

Americana Blues Resilience
▶ Stream
04

Born to Carry On

Bloodline and road. "Born to Carry On" channels the outlaw country tradition's fascination with inheritance — not money, but temperament. The way hardness and stubbornness and the refusal to quit get passed down through generations like a genetic fact. Stripped-back arrangement. Big emotional payload.

Outlaw Country Americana Heritage
▶ Stream
05

Burn What's Broken

Controlled destruction as a path forward. "Burn What's Broken" is dark country at its most philosophically interesting — a meditation on the necessity of letting things go, even when burning them is the hardest possible choice. The blues has always understood this paradox. So does this song.

Dark Country Southern Gothic Blues
▶ Stream
06

Coal Dust & Communion

Sacred and profane in the same breath. "Coal Dust & Communion" does what the best blues and dark country have always done: puts the church and the bar on the same street corner and watches what happens when they collide. This is a spiritually charged track with working-class grit embedded in every line. A standout Dark Country Boy song for fans of gothic Americana.

Gothic Americana Blues Spiritual
▶ Stream
07

Courage Ain't Free

One of the most direct tracks on the album. "Courage Ain't Free" doesn't romanticize bravery — it names what it costs. Gritty, unblinking, with the kind of lyrical directness that the best dark country and outlaw blues share. For anyone who's paid the price and knows the ledger doesn't always balance.

Outlaw Country Dark Blues Uncompromising
▶ Stream
08

Diesel & Grace

The working man's poem. "Diesel & Grace" finds beauty and even holiness in the workingman's life — the road, the machine, the grind. Americana at its most grounded, with a blues backbone that keeps it from getting too comfortable. One of the more melodically open tracks on the album; a good entry point for new Dark Country Boy listeners.

Americana Working Class Blues Country
▶ Stream
09

Don't Let the Fire Die

Both instruction and plea. "Don't Let the Fire Die" is one of the most personal tracks on the album — a direct address to whoever is listening to keep something alive: the music, the tradition, the fire inside themselves. The title echoes the album's central metaphor and gives it new weight this far into the record.

Dark Country Personal Outlaw Blues
▶ Stream
10

Every Man's War

The closing statement. "Every Man's War" ends the album with the recognition that struggle is both universal and deeply personal — it belongs to everyone and to no one else at the same time. Not a resolution, but a reckoning. The perfect closing track for an album that's never been interested in easy endings. Dark Country Boy at its most complete.
