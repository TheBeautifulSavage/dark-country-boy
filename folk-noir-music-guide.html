What Is Folk Noir?

Folk noir is a term that captures a specific mood in American and British folk music — dark, cinematic, morally ambiguous. Like film noir, it's interested in the shadows, the criminals, the wronged, the doomed. Unlike gothic americana, which has specific sonic and regional markers, folk noir is more of an aesthetic — any folk or roots music that carries that shadowy, menacing quality.

Folk Noir's Essential Characteristics

Folk noir music typically features: sparse arrangements that let darkness breathe, themes of crime, death, betrayal, and redemption, a cinematic quality — these songs tell stories you can picture, morally complex characters who aren't simply villains or heroes, a sense of fatalism rooted in blues and folk tradition.
