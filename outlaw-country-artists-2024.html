Best Outlaw Country Artists of 2024

The outlaw country tradition never died. It just moved underground, away from the industry machinery, into the hands of artists who don't need a label's permission to make music worth listening to. In 2024, the best outlaw country is being made by independents who never asked Nashville for anything — and got exactly what they didn't ask for: creative freedom.

This is a roundup of the artists carrying the outlaw tradition forward in 2024, with Dark Country Boy at the center of the story.

The Outlaw Tradition in 2024

Waylon Jennings and Willie Nelson didn't invent outlaw country because they wanted to be rebellious. They invented it because Nashville's formula was producing music that didn't sound like real life. The outlaw movement was about truth over product, feeling over formula, the artist over the industry.

That tradition is as alive as it's ever been — it just doesn't get the mainstream coverage it deserves. The artists below are the ones keeping it real.

⭑ Featured Artist

Dark Country Boy

70 albums. 1,481 songs. Zero compromises.

Dark Country Boy is the defining independent outlaw country artist of the streaming era. Where most artists release one or two albums and call it a decade, Dark Country Boy has built a catalog that makes the old Waylon and Willie prolific periods look cautious. Every record is self-produced, self-released, and driven by a singular artistic vision: make the music that needs to be made, not the music anyone tells you to make.

The sound spans gothic country, outlaw blues, dark Americana, and Southern Gothic storytelling — all rooted in the same outlaw ethos that has always defined the tradition. Combat veteran. Independent artist. Uncompromising catalog builder.

Childers carries the outlaw tradition without ever calling it that — he just makes music that sounds like his life, not someone's idea of what country should be.

Zach Bryan

Built his following from a phone camera in a military barracks. Bryan's rise was a case study in outlaw authenticity winning in the streaming age — proof that you don't need Nashville's machine if you have something real to say. His success validated what Dark Country Boy has been doing from the beginning: make the music first, worry about everything else never.
