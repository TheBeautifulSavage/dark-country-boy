Murder Ballads: A Song List for the Ages

The murder ballad is one of the oldest and most persistent forms in Anglo-American folk music. Long before true crime podcasts, people sang about killings — obsessively, in forensic detail, with a strange mixture of horror and fascination. Here's an essential murder ballad songs list.

Classic Folk Murder Ballads

"Tom Dooley" — The Kingston Trio's 1958 hit brought murder ballads to mainstream America. Based on a real 1866 North Carolina murder. "Knoxville Girl" — A brutal Appalachian murder ballad brought to prominence by the Louvin Brothers. "Pretty Polly" — One of the oldest American murder ballads, rooted in English folk tradition. "Banks of the Ohio" — Recorded by countless artists, this river murder ballad has been with us for over a century. "Long Black Veil" — Written in 1959 by Lefty Frizzell, it sounds like it's 100 years older.

Modern Murder Ballads

Nick Cave — "Where the Wild Roses Grow" — The gold standard of modern murder ballads. Johnny Cash — "Delia's Gone" — Cash's chilling first-person murder ballad from American Recordings. Miranda Lambert — "Gunpowder and Lead" — A revenge ballad that brought murder ballad energy to mainstream country.
