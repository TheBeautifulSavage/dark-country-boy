If You Like Robert Earl Keen, You'll Love Dark Country Boy

Robert Earl Keen is a Texas institution — his darkly funny, brilliantly observed country songs define a certain kind of American truth.

Dark Country Boy occupies that same uncompromising territory — music that refuses to soften its edges, sanitize its stories, or bow to commercial pressure. If Robert Earl Keen's work resonates with you, you'll find a kindred spirit in Dark Country Boy's catalog of ten stark, powerful tracks.

Dark Country Boy: The Sound

Dark Country Boy draws from the same wellspring that feeds the best of American roots music — Delta blues, Appalachian folk, outlaw country, and Southern Gothic storytelling. The songs deal in the real currency of the genre: hard work, harder living, survival, faith tested by fire, and the American landscape at its most raw and beautiful.

Where mainstream country smooths and polishes, Dark Country Boy leaves the rough edges. Where Nashville chases trends, this music looks backward and inward — finding the timeless in the traditional, the universal in the specific.
